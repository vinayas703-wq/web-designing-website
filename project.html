Kerala Traditional Foods

A Journey Through the Flavors of God's Own Country

Appam with Stew

A soft and fluffy pancake made from fermented rice batter, served with a creamy coconut-based stew.

Puttu and Kadala

Steamed cylinders of ground rice layered with coconut, typically served with a spicy black chickpea curry.

Karimeen Pollichathu

Pearl spot fish marinated in spices, wrapped in banana leaves, and pan-fried to perfection.

Palada Payasam

A sweet and rich dessert made with rice flakes, milk, and sugar, flavored with cardamom.

Kerala Beef Fry

Tender chunks of beef slow-roasted with aromatic spices, coconut slices, and curry leaves.

Malabar Biriyani

A flavorful and fragrant biriyani made with short-grain kaima rice, meat, and traditional spices.

Erissery

A traditional curry made with pumpkin, cowpeas, and a roasted coconut paste.

Avial

A thick mixture of various vegetables and coconut, seasoned with coconut oil and curry leaves.

Pazham Pori

Sweet ripe plantains coated in a seasoned flour batter and deep-fried to a golden crisp.

Kerala Parotta

A flaky, layered flatbread made from maida, popular across the state for its soft and crispy texture.
