Karimeen Pollichathu

Description

Karimeen Pollichathu is a star delicacy of the Kerala backwaters. Karimeen, or Pearl Spot fish, is the state fish of Kerala and is highly prized for its delicate, flaky meat. The term "Pollichathu" refers to the method of preparation where the marinated fish is pan-fried, coated with a thick, spicy masala, wrapped in a banana leaf, and then grilled or baked. This unique cooking method locks in the flavors and moisture, resulting in a dish that is extraordinarily aromatic and succulent.

The preparation begins with a simple marinade of turmeric, chili, and lemon juice, after which the fish is lightly shallow-fried. The magic happens with the secondary masala—a robust mixture of shallots, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, green chilies, and coconut milk, slowly roasted to perfection. When the fish, slathered in this masala and wrapped in a wilted banana leaf, hits the hot pan, the leaf imparts a subtle, earthy aroma to the entire dish. Opening the banana leaf parcel releases a burst of steam and spices that is truly intoxicating. It is an unforgettable culinary experience.

Benefits

  • Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Pearl spot fish is a great source of heart-healthy omega-3s.
  • Antioxidants: The rich masala uses spices like turmeric, which have strong anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Low Calorie: The baking/grilling method in a banana leaf requires less oil.
  • Protein Packed: Provides high-quality, easily digestible protein.

Instructions

Preparation: Clean the Karimeen and make deep slits on both sides. Marinate with turmeric, red chili powder, pepper, lemon juice, and salt. Leave for 30 minutes. Shallow fry the fish lightly in coconut oil for a few minutes on each side. Keep aside.

Making the Masala: In the same pan, add sliced shallots, ginger, garlic, green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté well. Add chopped tomatoes, red chili powder, coriander powder, and a dash of thick coconut milk to form a thick, cohesive masala paste. Wilt a banana leaf over an open flame to make it pliable. Spread a layer of the masala on the leaf, place the fried fish, and cover it with the remaining masala. Wrap the leaf tightly and tie it with a banana fiber string. Cook the parcel in a pan covered with a lid on low heat for 10-15 minutes, flipping once. Serve hot.
