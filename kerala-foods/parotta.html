Kerala Parotta

Description

Kerala Parotta (also known as Malabar Parotta) is a layered, flaky flatbread that holds a special place in the hearts of food lovers across Kerala and beyond. Unlike the North Indian Paratha which is made from whole wheat flour, the Kerala Parotta is primarily made using Maida (all-purpose flour). What makes the Kerala Parotta truly exceptional is its unique preparation process. The dough is kneaded to a soft, elastic consistency, rested, and then masterfully stretched and flipped in the air until it becomes paper-thin. This thin sheet of dough is pleated, coiled into a spiral shape, and then rolled out again before being cooked on a hot tawa (griddle).

The resulting flatbread is incredibly flaky, with distinct, pull-apart layers that are soft on the inside and delightfully crispy on the outside. Kerala Parotta is famously paired with rich, spicy curries, particularly Kerala Beef Fry, Chicken Curry, or vegetable Kurma. The layered texture of the parotta acts as a perfect vehicle to soak up the flavorful, aromatic gravies, making every bite a rich, satisfying, and indulgent experience.

Benefits

  • Energy Dense: A rich source of carbohydrates providing quick energy.
  • Versatile Pairing: Perfectly complements both vegetarian and non-vegetarian curries.
  • Comfort Food: Extremely satisfying and filling, acting as a great comfort meal.
  • Cultural Staple: A must-have dish representing the rich culinary tradition of Malabar.

Instructions

For Kerala Parotta: Mix all-purpose flour, a pinch of salt, sugar, and water to form a soft dough. Knead well until elastic, then add oil and let it rest for at least 2 hours. Divide the dough into small balls. Take a ball, flatten it, and stretch it out as thin as possible (like a translucent sheet) on an oiled surface. Gather the edges to form pleats, then coil it into a spiral (like a cinnamon roll). Let it rest again. Gently roll or press it out into a circle and cook on a hot tawa with oil or ghee until golden brown spots appear on both sides. Once cooked, crush the hot parotta gently from the sides using both hands to open up the flaky layers.
