Erissery

Description

Erissery is a classic, mild, and highly nutritious dish that holds a place of pride in the grand Kerala Sadhya (traditional vegetarian feast). It is a thick curry predominantly made with sweet pumpkin (mathanga) and red cowpeas (vanpayar). The dish perfectly balances the natural sweetness of the pumpkin with the earthy flavor of the cowpeas and the rich creaminess of coconut.

What sets Erissery apart is its signature tempering. While a paste of ground coconut, cumin, and green chilies forms the base of the curry, it is finished with a generous tempering of grated coconut roasted in coconut oil until it turns a deep, reddish-brown. This roasted coconut, along with mustard seeds and curry leaves, is poured over the cooked pumpkin and cowpeas. The resulting aroma of the roasted coconut defines the soul of this dish, making it a comforting and deeply satisfying part of the meal, usually served alongside rice and other curries.

Benefits

  • Rich in Vitamin A: Pumpkin is loaded with beta-carotene, essential for good vision.
  • High Dietary Fiber: Both cowpeas and pumpkin provide excellent fiber for digestive health.
  • Plant Protein: Red cowpeas are a fantastic source of vegetarian protein.
  • Low Spice Level: A gentle, soothing curry that is easy on the stomach.

Instructions

Preparation: Soak red cowpeas overnight. Pressure cook them until they are soft but not mushy. Peel and cut the sweet pumpkin into small cubes.

Cooking Process: Cook the pumpkin cubes with turmeric powder, red chili powder, salt, and a little water until they are tender and slightly mashed. Add the cooked cowpeas to the pumpkin. Grind freshly grated coconut with cumin seeds, garlic, and green chilies into a smooth paste. Add this paste to the pumpkin-cowpea mixture. Simmer for a few minutes until the raw smell of the coconut disappears. For the crucial tempering, heat coconut oil in a pan, splutter mustard seeds, add dried red chilies, curry leaves, and a handful of grated coconut. Roast until the coconut turns dark brown. Pour this over the curry and mix gently.
