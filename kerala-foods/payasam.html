Palada Payasam

Description

Palada Payasam is the crown jewel of Kerala desserts and an indispensable part of the traditional 'Sadhya' (feast) served during festivals like Onam and Vishu. It is a rich, creamy, and decadent sweet pudding made primarily from 'ada' (rice flakes), milk, and sugar. The slow and meticulous process of simmering the milk until it reduces and turns a beautiful pale pink color is what gives this payasam its extraordinary depth of flavor.

Unlike many other Indian desserts that heavily rely on a medley of spices, Palada Payasam is celebrated for its simplicity. The flavor profile is dominated by the pure, caramelized notes of reduced milk and sugar, with the soft, slippery rice ada providing a delightful textural contrast. Traditionally, it is slow-cooked over a wood fire in large, heavy-bottomed brass vessels known as 'Uruli'. A touch of cardamom and a generous garnish of ghee-roasted cashews and raisins add the perfect finishing touches to this heavenly dessert.

Benefits

  • Calcium Rich: Made with a large quantity of milk, it is a great source of calcium.
  • Energy Booster: The sugar and carbohydrates provide a quick source of energy.
  • Mood Elevator: The rich, sweet taste naturally triggers endorphins, making you feel happy.
  • Nutrient Dense: Cashews and raisins provide essential vitamins and minerals.

Instructions

Preparation: If using store-bought ada, boil them in water until soft, then wash them in cold water to remove excess starch. If making fresh ada, it involves making a rice flour batter, spreading it thinly on banana leaves, and steaming it.

Cooking the Payasam: In a heavy-bottomed pan, boil full-fat milk and reduce it slightly. Add the cooked ada to the simmering milk and let it cook on a low flame. As the milk reduces and thickens, add sugar. Continue to cook on low heat, stirring frequently to prevent burning at the bottom. The payasam is ready when the milk takes on a slightly pink hue and reaches a creamy consistency. Garnish with crushed cardamom and cashews/raisins roasted in ghee.
