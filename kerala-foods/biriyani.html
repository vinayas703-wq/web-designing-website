Malabar Biriyani

Description

The Malabar Biriyani, specifically the Thalassery Biriyani, is an iconic dish that represents the rich culinary influence of Arab traders on the Malabar coast of Kerala. Unlike the more common long-grain Basmati rice used in other biriyanis across India, Malabar Biriyani is distinguished by the use of a short-grain, highly aromatic rice variety known as Jeerakasala or Kaima rice. This unique rice absorbs the flavors of the meat and spices beautifully while maintaining a delicate texture.

The preparation process, known as the 'Dum' method, involves layering partially cooked spiced meat (usually chicken, mutton, or fish) with the aromatic Kaima rice. The pot is sealed tightly with dough and cooked over slow heat, often with hot coals placed on the lid. This traps the steam and aroma, allowing the flavors to meld together intimately. A generous garnish of fried onions (birista), roasted cashew nuts, and raisins provides a perfect sweet and crunchy contrast to the intensely spiced, moist rice and meat. It is a celebratory dish, synonymous with weddings and festivals in Northern Kerala.

Benefits

  • Balanced Meal: Provides a good mix of carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats.
  • Rich Spices: Spices like cloves and cardamom aid in digestion and have antioxidant properties.
  • Energy Source: The Jeerakasala rice is a great source of instant energy.
  • Comfort Food: The rich aroma and flavors act as an excellent mood booster.

Instructions

Preparation: Marinate the chicken pieces with ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, turmeric, coriander, and yogurt. Wash and drain the Jeerakasala rice.

Cooking process: Sauté thinly sliced onions until golden brown and crisp (birista), and roast cashews and raisins in ghee; set aside. For the masala, cook the marinated chicken with more onions, tomatoes, and a special biriyani masala powder until the gravy is thick and oil separates. In a separate pot, cook the rice with whole spices (cinnamon, cloves, cardamom) until it is 80% done. In a heavy-bottomed vessel, layer the chicken masala and the cooked rice. Sprinkle the fried onions, nuts, raisins, chopped coriander, mint leaves, and a few drops of rose water between layers. Seal the pot with dough and cook on "Dum" (low heat) for about 15-20 minutes. Mix gently before serving.
