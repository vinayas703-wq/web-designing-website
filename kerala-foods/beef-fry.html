Kerala Beef Fry

Description

Kerala Beef Fry, affectionately known as "Beef Ularthiyathu," is a legendary dish that evokes deep nostalgia and love among Keralites. It is a slow-roasted beef preparation characterized by its dark, rich color and intense, spicy flavor profile. The dish is a beautiful amalgamation of tender beef chunks, aromatic spices, and the unmistakable crunch of roasted coconut slices (thenga kothu). The pairing of Kerala Beef Fry with flaky Malabar Parotta or soft Appam is considered one of the ultimate culinary experiences in the region.

The preparation of Beef Ularthiyathu requires patience. The beef is first pressure cooked with spices to make it tender, and then it is slowly pan-roasted (ularthu) in coconut oil along with copious amounts of shallots, curry leaves, and a robust spice mix. The slow roasting process allows the moisture to evaporate, resulting in the spices clinging tightly to the meat, creating a deeply caramelized exterior and a melt-in-the-mouth interior. The liberal use of black pepper, fennel, and coconut oil gives it its signature flavor.

Benefits

  • High Protein: Beef is an excellent source of high-quality protein and essential amino acids.
  • Iron Rich: Red meat is one of the best dietary sources of easily absorbable heme iron.
  • Vitamin B12: Crucial for nerve function and the formation of red blood cells.
  • Metabolism Boost: The array of warm spices like black pepper can temporarily boost metabolism.

Instructions

Pressure Cooking: Cut beef into small cubes. Marinate with turmeric powder, coriander powder, chili powder, black pepper powder, garam masala, crushed ginger-garlic, vinegar, and salt. Pressure cook with very little water until the meat is tender but holds its shape.

Roasting (Ularthu): In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat coconut oil. Sauté thinly sliced coconut pieces (thenga kothu) until golden brown. Add plenty of sliced shallots, crushed garlic, green chilies, and a handful of curry leaves. Sauté until the shallots turn soft and slightly brown. Add the cooked beef along with any remaining stock. Cook on medium-low heat, stirring continuously, until all the liquid evaporates. Add more black pepper powder and a drizzle of coconut oil towards the end, and roast until the beef turns dark brown and is coated completely with the dry spice mixture.
