Puttu and Kadala

Description

Puttu and Kadala Curry is a quintessential Kerala breakfast that perfectly encapsulates the flavors of the region. Puttu is a cylindrical steamed rice cake made with coarsely ground rice flour and freshly grated coconut. The process of making puttu is an art in itself; the rice flour is roasted and then mixed with just enough water to resemble coarse breadcrumbs. It is then steamed in a special cylindrical vessel called a 'Puttu Kutti', often with alternating layers of coconut.

The mild, slightly sweet taste of the steamed puttu is brilliantly contrasted by the spicy, robust Kadala Curry. Kadala is a curry made with black chickpeas, roasted coconut, coriander, and an array of warm spices like fennel, cloves, and cinnamon. The dark, thick gravy of the Kadala curry gets its characteristic taste from the roasted coconut paste. When the crumbly puttu is mashed and soaked in this rich, spicy gravy, it creates a comforting and immensely satisfying meal. It is a hearty breakfast that provides sustained energy throughout the morning.

Benefits

  • High Protein: Black chickpeas are an excellent source of plant-based protein.
  • Complex Carbs: The rice flour provides complex carbohydrates for lasting energy.
  • Rich in Fiber: Both the chickpeas and grated coconut add significant dietary fiber.
  • Steamed Goodness: Because puttu is steamed, it requires almost zero oil, making it very healthy.

Instructions

For Puttu: Take roasted rice flour and gradually mix in water and salt until the mixture forms coarse, moist crumbs (it should hold shape when pressed in the palm but crumble easily). In a puttu maker, add a small layer of grated coconut, then a handful of the rice flour mix, and repeat until full. Steam for about 5-8 minutes until steam escapes from the top.

For Kadala Curry: Soak black chickpeas overnight and pressure cook them until soft. Roast grated coconut with coriander powder, chili powder, turmeric, and fennel seeds until dark brown, then grind to a smooth paste. Sauté onions, tomatoes, ginger, and garlic, add the cooked chickpeas and the roasted coconut paste. Simmer until the gravy thickens and garnish with mustard seeds and curry leaves tempered in coconut oil.
