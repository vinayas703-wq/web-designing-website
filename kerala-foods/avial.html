Avial

Description

Avial is the quintessential Kerala vegetable medley, a dish so deeply rooted in the culture that a Sadhya is considered incomplete without it. It is a thick, semi-dry preparation consisting of an assortment of locally grown vegetables like drumsticks, yams, raw plantains, carrots, ash gourd, and snake gourd, all cut into uniform, thick batons. The visual appeal of Avial lies in this colorful array of vegetables.

The cooking technique involves boiling these vegetables with a pinch of turmeric until tender. The flavor profile is built around a coarsely ground paste of fresh coconut, cumin seeds, and green chilies. The defining characteristics of Avial, however, are its slight tartness—traditionally achieved by adding sour yogurt (or sometimes raw mango or tamarind)—and the final, generous drizzle of raw coconut oil and fresh curry leaves over the warm dish. This combination creates a fresh, fragrant, and profoundly satisfying dish that celebrates the bounty of nature.

Benefits

  • Nutrient Powerhouse: A single dish provides a wide spectrum of vitamins and minerals from various vegetables.
  • Probiotics: The addition of yogurt aids in maintaining healthy gut flora.
  • Healthy Fats: Raw coconut oil is known for its beneficial medium-chain fatty acids.
  • High Fiber: The mixed vegetables ensure a very high dietary fiber intake.

Instructions

Preparation: Wash, peel, and cut all vegetables (drumstick, carrot, beans, raw banana, ash gourd, yam) into 2-inch long thick strips. Grind fresh coconut, cumin seeds, and green chilies into a coarse paste without adding too much water.

Cooking Process: In a heavy-bottomed vessel, add the vegetables (hardest ones first, like yam, followed by others). Add a little water, turmeric powder, and salt. Cover and cook until the vegetables are just tender, ensuring they retain their shape and don't turn mushy. Add the coarsely ground coconut paste to the cooked vegetables and mix gently. Simmer for a minute. Remove from heat. Once the heat slightly reduces, add whisked sour yogurt and mix well. Finally, drizzle a tablespoon of raw coconut oil and garnish with fresh curry leaves. Keep covered for the flavors to steep before serving.
