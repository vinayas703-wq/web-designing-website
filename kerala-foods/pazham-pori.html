Pazham Pori

Description

Pazham Pori, also known as Ethakka Appam, is the undisputed king of Kerala snacks. Walk into any local tea shop (chayakada) in Kerala at 4 PM, and you will find these golden, crispy banana fritters proudly displayed in a glass case. It is an incredibly simple yet profoundly comforting snack that relies entirely on the quality of its star ingredient: the Nendran banana (Ethapazham), a variety indigenous to Kerala.

To make the perfect Pazham Pori, one needs overripe Nendran bananas—the ones with black spots on their skin, indicating maximum sweetness and softness. The bananas are sliced longitudinally, dipped in a mildly sweetened batter made primarily of all-purpose flour (maida) with a hint of turmeric for color, and then deep-fried in coconut oil. The result is a magical contrast of textures and flavors: a crispy, golden, slightly salty outer shell that gives way to a hot, sweet, meltingly soft banana interior. Paired with a hot cup of strong Kerala tea, it is pure bliss.

Benefits

  • Instant Energy: Ripe bananas provide natural sugars for a quick energy boost.
  • Potassium Rich: Bananas are an excellent source of potassium, vital for heart health.
  • Mood Elevator: The combination of sweet banana and crispy texture is an ultimate comfort food.
  • Satiating: A filling snack that keeps hunger pangs at bay.

Instructions

Preparation: Select very ripe Nendran bananas. Peel them and slice them vertically into thin strips (about 3-4 slices per banana).

Making the Batter: In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour (maida), a tablespoon of rice flour (for crispiness), a pinch of turmeric powder (for the golden color), a pinch of salt, and a little sugar. Gradually add water to form a smooth, lump-free batter of coating consistency (neither too thick nor too watery).

Frying: Heat coconut oil in a deep frying pan or wok (cheenachatti). Dip each banana slice into the batter, ensuring it is evenly coated on all sides. Gently drop it into the hot oil. Fry on medium heat until both sides turn a crisp, golden yellow. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels. Serve hot with tea.
